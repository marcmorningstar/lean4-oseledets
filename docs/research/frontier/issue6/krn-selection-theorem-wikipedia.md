From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

In mathematics, the **Kuratowski–Ryll-Nardzewski measurable selection theorem** is a result from [measure theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Measure_theory "Measure theory")
 that gives a sufficient condition for a [set-valued function](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Set-valued_function "Set-valued function")
 to have a measurable [selection function](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Selection_function "Selection function")
.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuratowski_and_Ryll-Nardzewski_measurable_selection_theorem#cite_note-1)
[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuratowski_and_Ryll-Nardzewski_measurable_selection_theorem#cite_note-2)
[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuratowski_and_Ryll-Nardzewski_measurable_selection_theorem#cite_note-3)
 It is named after the Polish mathematicians [Kazimierz Kuratowski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kazimierz_Kuratowski "Kazimierz Kuratowski")
 and [Czesław Ryll-Nardzewski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Czes%C5%82aw_Ryll-Nardzewski "Czesław Ryll-Nardzewski")
.[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuratowski_and_Ryll-Nardzewski_measurable_selection_theorem#cite_note-4)

Many classical selection results follow from this theorem[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuratowski_and_Ryll-Nardzewski_measurable_selection_theorem#cite_note-5)
 and it is widely used in [mathematical economics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematical_economics "Mathematical economics")
 and [optimal control](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optimal_control "Optimal control")
.[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuratowski_and_Ryll-Nardzewski_measurable_selection_theorem#cite_note-6)

Statement of the theorem
------------------------

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Kuratowski_and_Ryll-Nardzewski_measurable_selection_theorem&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Statement of the theorem")\
\]

Let X {\\displaystyle X} ![{\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) be a [Polish space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polish_space "Polish space")
, B ( X ) {\\displaystyle {\\mathcal {B}}(X)} ![{\displaystyle {\mathcal {B}}(X)}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/0c7b211c3edfb7916956743e616087b5ac0e6dd0) the [Borel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borel_set "Borel set")
 [σ-algebra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sigma-algebra "Sigma-algebra")
 of X {\\displaystyle X} ![{\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) , ( Ω , F ) {\\displaystyle (\\Omega ,{\\mathcal {F}})} ![{\displaystyle (\Omega ,{\mathcal {F}})}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4af05190f6fb10d1eec88b5f66f940dc88b5ec87) a [measurable space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Measurable_space "Measurable space")
 and ψ {\\displaystyle \\psi } ![{\displaystyle \psi }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/45e5789e5d9c8f7c79744f43ecaaf8ba42a8553a) a multifunction on Ω {\\displaystyle \\Omega } ![{\displaystyle \Omega }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/24b0d5ca6f381068d756f6337c08e0af9d1eeb6f) taking values in the set of nonempty closed subsets of X {\\displaystyle X} ![{\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) .

Suppose that ψ {\\displaystyle \\psi } ![{\displaystyle \psi }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/45e5789e5d9c8f7c79744f43ecaaf8ba42a8553a) is F {\\displaystyle {\\mathcal {F}}} ![{\displaystyle {\mathcal {F}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/205d4b91000d9dcf1a5bbabdfa6a8395fa60b676) \-weakly measurable, that is, for every open subset U {\\displaystyle U} ![{\displaystyle U}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/458a728f53b9a0274f059cd695e067c430956025) of X {\\displaystyle X} ![{\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) , we have

{ ω : ψ ( ω ) ∩ U ≠ ∅ } ∈ F . {\\displaystyle \\{\\omega :\\psi (\\omega )\\cap U\\neq \\emptyset \\}\\in {\\mathcal {F}}.} ![{\displaystyle \{\omega :\psi (\omega )\cap U\neq \emptyset \}\in {\mathcal {F}}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/e98f18b1d370afd038cfffba4377441077a0a8b7) 

Then ψ {\\displaystyle \\psi } ![{\displaystyle \psi }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/45e5789e5d9c8f7c79744f43ecaaf8ba42a8553a) has a [selection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Choice_function "Choice function")
 that is F {\\displaystyle {\\mathcal {F}}} ![{\displaystyle {\mathcal {F}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/205d4b91000d9dcf1a5bbabdfa6a8395fa60b676) \- B ( X ) {\\displaystyle {\\mathcal {B}}(X)} ![{\displaystyle {\mathcal {B}}(X)}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/0c7b211c3edfb7916956743e616087b5ac0e6dd0) \-measurable.[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuratowski_and_Ryll-Nardzewski_measurable_selection_theorem#cite_note-7)
